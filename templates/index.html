How it works (Character Autoencoder):
  • The model encodes each word into a 128-dimensional latent space
  • It creates a 10√ó10 grid using parallelogram interpolation in latent space
  • Grid corners: [0,0]=Word1, [max,0]=Word2, [0,max]=Word3, [max,max]=Word2+Word3-Word1
  • Formula: point[i,j] = Word1 + (i/max)*(Word2-Word1) + (j/max)*(Word3-Word1)
  • Each point is decoded back to generate intermediate word forms
  • Only lowercase letters (a-z) supported, max 10 characters for autoencoder

The Wobbly Line Hypothesis: When we decode interpolated points (autoencoder) or find nearest neighbors (GloVe/Word2Vec), we're rounding to discrete outputs. This means the actual decoded/quantized points don't form a perfect parallelogram in latent space.

Char-Level Autoencoder: The visualization shows the ideal parallelogram as a green semi-transparent surface mesh. The red points show where the decoded words actually land when re-encoded - this is the "wobble". The 3 yellow spheres are your input words, and the magenta sphere is the computed 4th corner.
